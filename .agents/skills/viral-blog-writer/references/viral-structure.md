# Viral Structure

Use this structure by default:

## 1. Hook fast

Open with one of:

- A surprising fact
- A sharp reversal
- A high-stakes implication
- A clean "everyone thinks X, but actually Y" line

## 2. Explain the shift

Answer:

- What happened
- Why now
- Why people should care

## 3. Build tension

Show the gap between expectation and reality.

Examples:

- Faster but less reliable
- Cheaper but strategically risky
- More capable but harder to trust

## 4. Make it practical

Translate the topic into actions, decisions, or changed assumptions for the reader.

## 5. Close with conviction

End with one clear takeaway, prediction, or decision frame.

## Style markers

- Short paragraphs
- Strong subheads
- Minimal throat-clearing
- Specific nouns over abstract buzzwords
- Measured confidence instead of hype
