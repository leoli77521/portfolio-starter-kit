# Voice And Relevance Guide

Use this guide before any public submission.

## What Good Looks Like

- The comment reacts to one real point from the target page.
- The wording sounds like a practitioner, not a template.
- The site mention is light and secondary.
- The linked destination is the closest fit: article first, then hub, guide, blog, homepage.

## Human-Sounding Checklist

- Mention one concrete idea, example, tradeoff, or claim from the page.
- Add one short extension, comparison, or observation from a builder's point of view.
- Keep the brand mention to one brief clause at most.
- Vary your opener, sentence length, and cadence across a batch.
- If the page gives you nothing specific to react to, skip it.

## Avoid

- "Great post" with no follow-up detail
- repetitive compliments
- generic SEO language
- exact-match anchor spam
- comment-body links when the website field is enough
- acting like every page deserves a backlink

## Better Comment Shape

1. Open with one specific point from the page.
2. Add one useful extension or practitioner reaction.
3. If needed, lightly mention that your site covers a related angle.

## Example

Good:

```text
The section on permission visibility is the part that stood out to me. A lot of AI tooling feels impressive in demos until you have to expose tool actions and trust boundaries to real users. We write about similar coding-agent and MCP workflow questions at ToLearn, so this was a useful framing.
```

Weak:

```text
Great article. Very informative and useful. I also run a blog about AI and would love to share it here because readers may benefit.
```

## Destination Bias

- Use an article URL when the external page and your article are tightly aligned.
- Use a topic hub or guide when the external page is broader than one post.
- Use the homepage only when the comment is genuinely site-level.
